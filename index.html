Prevalence of Smoking and its Impacts

Background Information and Findings

Smoking causes many health complications such as increased risk of cancer, heart disease, lung complications, and strokes. Approximately 1 out of 10 Australians smoke on a daily basis. To illustrate the impact smoking has on Australians, the bubble plot reflects that states with higher rates of daily smokers have a life expectancy lower than the national average of 83. These states include Tasmania, Queensland, and the Northern Territory. This concludes that higher levels of smoking reduces life expectancy.
